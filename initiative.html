About the Initiative

Understanding our mission, methodology, and the team behind The Agentic State

A collaborative effort to shape the future of AI in government

OUR MISSION

The Agentic State initiative emerged from a recognition that the rapid advancement of AI technology requires thoughtful, evidence-based guidance for governments worldwide. Our mission is to provide comprehensive research and practical recommendations that help public sector leaders navigate the complex landscape of AI adoption.

We believe that AI has the potential to dramatically improve government efficiency, citizen satisfaction, and democratic participation. However, realizing this potential requires careful consideration of ethical implications, governance frameworks, and implementation strategies that prioritize public trust and human oversight.

Through rigorous research, stakeholder engagement, and international collaboration, we aim to establish best practices that ensure AI serves as a force for good in public administration.

RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

Our research combines quantitative analysis of AI adoption patterns with qualitative insights from government practitioners, technology experts, and civil society representatives. We conducted over 200 interviews across 15 countries, analyzed 50+ case studies of AI implementation in government, and reviewed 300+ academic papers and policy documents.

The methodology follows a three-phase approach: discovery (identifying key trends and challenges), analysis (deep-diving into specific use cases and outcomes), and synthesis (developing actionable recommendations and frameworks). Each phase involved extensive peer review and stakeholder validation.

We also established an international advisory board comprising former government officials, AI ethics researchers, and technology policy experts to ensure the rigor and relevance of our findings.

CORE TEAM

Marcus Rodriguez - Governance and Ethics Specialist with expertise in AI accountability frameworks. Former advisor to the European Commission on AI regulation and digital rights.

Dr. Elena Petrova - Technical Director specializing in large language models and autonomous systems. Led AI implementation projects for multiple government agencies across Europe and North America.

ADVISORY BOARD

Our international advisory board brings together diverse perspectives from government, academia, civil society, and industry. Members include former heads of digital government agencies, leading AI researchers, human rights advocates, and technology policy experts.

The board provides strategic guidance, validates research findings, and helps ensure our recommendations are practical, ethical, and globally relevant. They also facilitate connections with key stakeholders and help disseminate our findings to decision-makers worldwide.

PROJECT TIMELINE

Phase 1 (Q1-Q2 2024): Research and stakeholder engagement across 15 countries, including government interviews, case study analysis, and literature review.

Phase 2 (Q3 2024): Analysis and synthesis of findings, development of recommendations, and peer review process with international experts.

Phase 3 (Q4 2024): Publication of the comprehensive whitepaper, launch of implementation toolkit, and establishment of practitioner community.

PARTNERSHIPS

The initiative is supported by leading institutions in digital government and AI policy, including the OECD Observatory of Public Sector Innovation, the World Bank Digital Development Practice, and the Alan Turing Institute. These partnerships provide access to global networks, additional research resources, and platforms for disseminating our findings.

We also collaborate with government innovation labs, technology companies, and civil society organizations to ensure our research addresses real-world challenges and opportunities. These collaborations help us stay current with emerging technologies and implementation experiences.
